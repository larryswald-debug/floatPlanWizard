Why float plans matter

A float plan is a simple safety agreement: if you don’t return, someone knows exactly where you intended to go, who is with you, and when to start calling for help. The clearer the plan, the less time is lost to guessing.

The danger isn’t the plan—it’s the gap.

Many situations start as ordinary problems: weather changes, mechanical issues, an unexpected stop, a dead phone. When a return window passes, your float plan becomes the one place your designated contact can turn for clear, actionable information.

Time matters When a return window is missed, delays often come from incomplete or unclear details.
Details matter Route, waypoints, vessel description, passenger list, and contact info should be immediately visible.
Clarity matters Your plan should be readable by a person under pressure—not buried in scattered notes.
Handoff matters Your designated contact should know what to do next, and when to escalate.
